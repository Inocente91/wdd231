Wax Museum

Primera núm. 8281 Centro CP 22000 Tijuana, Tijuana, Baja California

Inaugurated in 1993, the Tijuana Wax Museum is considered the second of its kind in all of Latin America, featuring more than 80 replicas of historical figures, artists, and legends from film and television.

Municipal Auditorium

Blvd. Gustavo Díaz Ordaz s/n Fracc. El Paraíso CP 22220, Tijuana, Baja California

The Auditorio Municipal Fausto Gutiérrez Moreno, commonly known as the Tijuana Municipal Auditorium, is a prominent indoor venue located in Tijuana, Baja California, Mexico. Established in 1969, it has a seating capacity of approximately 4,500 and serves as a hub for various events, including concerts, sports, and cultural performances.

CECUT

Blvd. Gustavo Díaz Ordaz s/n Fracc. El Paraíso CP 22220, Tijuana, Baja California

The CECUT is a cultural center in Mexico, located near the border, founded on October 20, 1982. It is the institution that offers the most extensive and diverse cultural programming in the northwest region and the only infrastructure of the Ministry of Culture—formerly the National Council for Culture and the Arts—outside the country’s capital. It is committed to meeting the artistic and cultural needs of the population through programs that include various artistic expressions and topics related to both traditional and contemporary culture.

Rosarito Beach

Blvd. Benito Juárez 31, Centro, 22710 Rosarito, B.C., Mexico

Rosarito Beach, located approximately 40 minutes south of the U.S.-Mexico border, is a popular coastal destination in Baja California, Mexico. Known for its beautiful sandy shores, vibrant nightlife, and rich cultural heritage, it attracts both locals and tourists seeking a beach getaway.
